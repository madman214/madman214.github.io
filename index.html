Introduction

Our team used the COVID-19 and its impact on education, social life and mental health of students: A Survey report to analyze relationships between age, time spent in online class and rating, time spent on sleep, weight change, and multiple other factors to determine the impact of the pandemic on 1182 students.

So, we took the given data and performed comparisons and visualizations to determine whether correlations were positive or negative and whether certain factors influenced others more than the rest.

In the end, all of our visualizations were created with the purpose to determine how students' mental health changed over the pandemic and how them and their habits changed.

Hypothesis

  • The quarantines resulting from the COVID-19 pandemic had an overall negative effect on the population of New Delhi.

Data Description

  • COVID-19 and Its Impact on Students: This dataset compiles results from a cross-sectional survey conducted with a sample size of 1182 students from different age groups and different educational institutions in the Delhi National Capital Region (NCR). [https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/kunal28chaturvedi/covid19-and-its-impact-on-students]

Conclusion

  • From the trendline we can conclude that as time spent on studying increased, time spent on social media decreased.

  • From the plotâ€™s trendline we can conclude that there was an overall negative correlation between the age of the subjects and time spent on sleep. This means that as the age increased, the time spent on sleep slowly decreased.

  • From the data we can conclude that the more fitness a student does, their weight changes or stays the same and those that didnâ€™t do as much gained weight.

  • From the data we can conclude that the size of the device seemed to change the rating of their experience, the lowest rating was for a smartphone, which has a small screen, online experience with a tablet was higher rated, possibly because it has a larger screen, and the highest rated were people who used a laptop/desktop and smartphone. The only outlier to this conclusion is only the laptop/desktop, but that could be explained by not many people using laptops/desktops in the modern day.

  • From this data we can conclude that there is no correlation between the time spent in online class and weight, although there are other factors that couldâ€™ve affected the results such as time spent on fitness
